# Example Session: Career Decision

> **Context**: A developer wants to quit their job to become a freelancer.  
> **Skill**: Maieutics v2.0  
> **Outcome**: The user discovers their "freedom" narrative hides unexamined risks and undefined success criteria.

---

## Phase 1: Conceptual Clarification

**User**: I'm miserable at my job. I should quit and go freelance — I'll have freedom, make more money, and be happier.

**Agent**: When you say "freedom," what specifically are you lacking now that freelancing would provide? And when you say "make more money," do you mean higher hourly rate, higher total income, or more income stability?

**User**: Freedom means I can choose what projects I work on and set my own schedule. And I mean higher total income — I think I can charge more as a freelancer than my salary.

> ✅ *Exit condition met*: "Freedom" = project choice + schedule control, "more money" = higher total annual income.

---

## Phase 2: Premise Excavation

**Agent**: For "quitting to freelance" to be the right move, there must be an assumption that your unhappiness is caused by employment itself, not by something else — your specific company, your role, your commute, or your work-life boundaries. Is that an assumption you've tested?

**User**: I... haven't really thought about that. I just assumed it's the job structure.

**Agent**: There's another hidden premise: that you can reliably find clients who will pay more than your current salary, and that the time you spend finding clients won't cancel out the income gains. What are you basing that on?

**User**: I have one friend who freelances and seems to do well. But I don't actually know his numbers.

> ✅ *Exit condition met*: Two hidden premises surfaced — (1) unhappiness is caused by employment structure [untested], (2) freelance income will exceed current salary net of business development time [unverified, based on anecdote].

---

## Phase 3: Logical Stress-Testing

### Counterexample Probe

**Agent**: Can you imagine a scenario where you quit, become a freelancer, have full project choice and schedule control, but you're more miserable than you are now?

**User**: I guess if I can't find clients, or if I have to take terrible projects just to pay rent, or if I miss having colleagues.

### Boundary Extremes

**Agent**: If we push "freedom" to its extreme — you have zero clients, total schedule control, but no income — does your reasoning still hold?

**User**: No, that's just unemployment, not freedom.

**Agent**: So there's a boundary. Freedom is valuable only above some income threshold. Where is that threshold for you?

**User**: I... don't know. I haven't calculated my minimum viable income.

### Necessity vs. Sufficiency

**Agent**: Is quitting your job necessary to get more freedom, or merely one way? Could you negotiate remote work, project choice, or a sabbatical at your current job?

**User**: I haven't tried any of that. I just assumed it was impossible.

> ✅ *Exit condition met*: User acknowledged multiple logical tensions — freedom has an income boundary, alternative paths exist, unhappiness source is unverified.

---

## Phase 4: Information Gap Mapping

**Agent**: Let's map the unknowns. What would you need to know to make this decision with confidence?

**User**: I'd need to know: my minimum monthly expenses, how many hours I'd spend on business development, what rates I could actually charge, how consistent the work would be, and whether my current job would negotiate.

**Agent**: Which of these are closable with research or conversation, and which are inherent uncertainties you can't eliminate?

**User**: The expenses are closable — I can calculate them. The negotiation is closable — I can ask. The rates and consistency are guesses — I'd need to test the market. The business development time is an inherent uncertainty until I actually do it.

> ✅ *Exit condition met*: Gaps identified and classified — 2 closable (expenses, negotiation), 2 testable (rates, consistency), 1 inherent uncertainty (time investment).

---

## Phase 5: Aporetic Summary

**Agent**: Here's the map of your reasoning as it stands:

**Conclusion**: Quit job → freelance → freedom + more money + happiness.

**Premises**:
1. Employment structure causes unhappiness → [Unverified — could be specific company/role]
2. Freelance income > current salary net of business development → [Unverified — based on one anecdote]
3. Freedom (project choice + schedule) is the missing ingredient → [Unverified — boundaries undefined]

**Hidden Premises Surfaced**:
- Unhappiness is structural, not situational
- Freelancing guarantees better outcomes
- Current job is inflexible (negotiation not attempted)
- One friend's experience is representative

**Logical Tensions**:
- Freedom without income is unemployment, not liberation
- Alternative paths to freedom exist within current employment
- Income threshold for "freedom" is undefined

**Information Gaps**:
| Gap | Classification |
|-----|---------------|
| Minimum viable monthly income | Closable — calculate expenses |
| Current employer flexibility | Closable — initiate negotiation |
| Market rates for skills | Testable — interview/propose |
| Work consistency | Testable — pilot with side projects |
| Business development time burden | Inherent uncertainty — only knowable by doing |

**Agent**: What do you see now that you didn't see before?

**User**: I see that I was running away from something I don't fully understand, toward something I haven't actually researched. I need to test the waters before jumping.

> ✅ *Exit condition met*: User achieved autonomous clarity. Maieutic act complete.

---

## Post-Session Notes

**What happened**:
- The user entered with an emotionally charged decision ("I'm miserable, I should quit")
- Maieutics separated the emotional state from the structural analysis
- The user discovered they had made zero attempts to improve their current situation before deciding to abandon it

**What did NOT happen**:
- The agent never said "don't quit your job" or "freelancing is risky"
- The agent never provided financial planning advice or market rate data
- The agent never validated the user's misery as "real" or "imagined"

**Key insight**: The user moved from "escape" to "exploration." They will likely make a better decision now, not because they were told what to do, but because they can see their own reasoning clearly.
